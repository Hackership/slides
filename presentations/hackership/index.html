Does this really work?

Hackerschool New York kinda does it already - in their third year now. But more as a assessment center for companies while this is really from hackers for hackers by hackers.

But yes, the programme itself is proven to work

The 15-of-1st support model

A hackership is run on the basic idea that people acquire many more skills and with them are offered better positions and jobs. That's why every participant pledges at the beginning of the programme to support it with 15% of their first year salary over the course of 12 months after the programme is over for them.

With this money the OpenTechSchool is able to continuously run this and other tech education programmes.

Why not a real hire-fee?

First, the learners can only choose from a limited pool of companies and if their position of choice is not under them, what then? Secondly, this is a programme from hackers for hackers to get better at hacking and not to produce factory workers for some companies.

The only interest we want to have in mind is the one of the hacker themselves.

But can companies...?

Well, if a hacker was accepted and their employer is willing to pay the fee, fine with us. But we require a signed letter from them stating they won't distract the hacker during the programme. And even if we send them the invoice to a third party, our contract and our interest lies with the hacker themself only.

Btw. some employers pay their employees a 70% salary for half a year (also through the programme) and this way cover the 15-of-1st. Maybe that's a model to discuss with your employer.
